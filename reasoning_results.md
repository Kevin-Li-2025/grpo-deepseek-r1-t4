### Question 2: Bakery Profit Problem
*   **Prompt**: "A bakery sells cakes for $15 each. If they sell 8 cakes and spend $45 on ingredients, what is their net profit?"
*   **Model Response**:
```html
To determine the net profit of the bakery, we need to follow these steps:

1. Calculate the total revenue from selling the cakes.
2. Subtract the cost of the ingredients from the total revenue to find the net profit.

Step 1: Calculate the total revenue.
The bakery sells each cake for $15 and they sell 8 cakes. Therefore, the total revenue is:
\[ 15 \text{ dollars/cake} \times 8 \text{ cakes} = 120 \text{ dollars} \]

Step 2: Subtract the cost of the ingredients from the total revenue.
The cost of the ingredients is $45. Therefore, the net profit is:
\[ 120 \text{ dollars} - 45 \text{ dollars} = 75 \text{ dollars} \]

So, the net profit of the bakery is \(\boxed{75}\).
```

### Question 3: Algebra Equation
*   **Prompt**: "Solve for x: 3x + 7 = 22. Provide the numeric value."
*   **Model Response**:
```html
To solve the equation \(3x + 7 = 22\) for \(x\), we will follow these steps:

1. **Isolate the term with the variable \(x\)** by subtracting 7 from both sides of the equation.
2. **Solve for \(x\)** by dividing both sides of the equation by 3.

Let's go through these steps in detail:

1. Start with the original equation:
   \[
   3x + 7 = 22
   \]

2. Subtract 7 from both sides to isolate the term with \(x\):
   \[
   3x + 7 - 7 = 22 - 7
   \]
   Simplifying both sides, we get:
   \[
   3x = 15
   \]

3. Divide both sides by 3 to solve for \(x\):
   \[
   \frac{3x}{3} = \frac{15}{3}
   \]
   Simplifying both sides, we get:
   \[
   x = 5
   \]

So, the solution to the equation \(3x + 7 = 22\) is \(\boxed{5}\).
```
